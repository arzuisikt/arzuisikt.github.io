About Me

I am a PhD student in Finance at the Haskayne School of Business, University of Calgary. My research examines how information, financial technology, artificial intelligence, and digital platform design influence investor beliefs, risk-taking, and financial decision-making. I am particularly interested in financial information, gamification, AI-human interaction, and market design.

My work combines experimental methods, financial economics, and data science to study how people respond to information, technology, and artificial intelligence in financial markets.

Before starting my PhD, I completed graduate studies in Data Science and Artificial Intelligence at Harvard University and earned my bachelor’s degree in Economics from Boğaziçi University.

Research

Does Paying for Data Change Investment Decisions?

With Philipp Chapkovski, Mariana Khapko, and Marius Zoican

This project examines whether paying for financial information changes investor beliefs, forecasts, and portfolio decisions relative to receiving the same information for free.

Working Paper

Does Trading Gamification Fuel Bubbles?

With Debaleena Goswami and Marius Zoican

This project studies whether gamified trading environments increase risk-taking, affect price formation, and contribute to asset-price bubbles.

In Progress

Can Gamification Reduce Financial Anxiety?

With Mariana Khapko, Mircea Matei, Mihai Toma, and Marius Zoican

This project explores whether gamification can reduce financial anxiety and support engagement with financial education and decision-making.
